I've spent years working deep in the stack with PostgreSQL and SQL Server — tuning slow queries, diagnosing production outages, designing indexing strategies for high-traffic systems, and helping engineering teams understand what their databases are actually doing under load.

Everything I build here is rooted in those real-world situations. The tools exist because I needed them. The books are written the way I wish they had been when I was learning — direct, technical, no filler. The blog covers the problems that actually come up in production, not the ones that make good tutorials.

My philosophy is simple: your database tools should run where your database runs. No cloud connectivity required. No uploading query plans to someone else's server. Every tool here is self-hosted and stays on your infrastructure.

If you work with databases professionally — whether you're a DBA, a backend engineer, or somewhere in between — you'll find something here that saves you time, makes problems easier to diagnose, or helps you understand a concept you've been glossing over.
